From the Big Bang to the Black Holes

The Big Bang

The Big Bang is the very starting point of the universe! At this point, we do not know what happened—natural laws begin to break down. We may never know, but we do know that the universe as we know it started here!

The Expansion of the Universe

Einstein's theory of relativity along with Edwin Hubble's discovery proved the universe is expanding. From hot, dense elementary particles, the universe expands to form everything around us!

Matter

Within just one second, the universe had expanded and cooled enough for the first element to form—the hydrogen atom!

The Birth of Light

As time passed and the universe continued expanding, hydrogen atoms began to clump together. Gravity compressed them until stars were born.

Death of the First Generation of Stars

Even stars come to an end. Their explosive deaths scattered the elements that formed all terrestrial objects—including our solar system and Earth. These events also triggered the birth of new stars.

When a Star Dies...

Depending on its size, a star's death may lead to:

A Neutron Star

White Dwarf
